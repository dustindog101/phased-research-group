# 13 — Copywriting Guide

## Why This Matters

Reddit users in the peptide community are **hyper-aware** of AI-generated copy. They actively flag it as a scam signal. If your site reads like ChatGPT wrote it, they assume:
1. You're lazy
2. You're probably doing "AI engine optimization" (spamming Reddit to manipulate ChatGPT/Google AI results)
3. You might be a scam

This is not theoretical. There are active Reddit threads documenting peptide companies doing exactly this.

## What to Avoid (AI-Sounding Patterns)

### Em Dashes (—)
- **Rule**: Maximum ONE em dash per page. Zero is better.
- **Why**: ChatGPT overuses them. Reddit users count them.
- **Fix**: Use periods, commas, or colons instead.
- ❌ "Precision synthesis — verified purity — scientific integrity"
- ✅ "Precision synthesis. Verified purity. Scientific integrity."

### AI Buzzwords and Phrases
Never use these:
- "Delve into"
- "In today's world"
- "When it comes to"
- "It's worth noting"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Revolutionary formula"
- "Game-changing"
- "World-class"
- "Fuel the breakthroughs of tomorrow"
- "Step into the future of science"
- "The partner you've been waiting for"
- "This is where serious researchers come to win"
- "Pride ourselves on"
- "In the realm of"
- "Navigate the complexities"
- "Seamless" (overused)
- "Cutting-edge"
- "State-of-the-art"
- "Robust" (unless describing data)

### Empty Positioning Claims
- ❌ "Tired of me-too suppliers? We're not like them."
- ❌ "We're setting a new benchmark for excellence."
- ✅ Say what you actually do differently (specific, not comparative).

### Overly Formal Sentence Structure
- ❌ "Our products are subjected to rigorous analytical testing protocols to ensure compliance with industry standards."
- ✅ "Every batch gets HPLC and mass-spec testing by a third-party lab."

### Excessive Hedging
- ❌ "Our peptides may potentially offer benefits that could possibly assist with..."
- ✅ "Our peptides are for research use only. We don't make claims about what they do."

### Generic Marketing Speak
- ❌ "Experience the PRG difference"
- ❌ "Elevate your research"
- ❌ "Discover the power of"
- ✅ State facts. Let the reader draw conclusions.

### Tri-Card "Innovation / Implementation / Precision" Layouts
Reddit users specifically mock this pattern. It screams AI-generated. Avoid the 3-card "pillars" layout unless each card has specific, non-generic content.

### Bullet Points Where Paragraphs Would Be More Natural
AI loves bullet lists. Use them when appropriate, but don't bullet everything. Some things should be paragraphs.

## What to Do (Human-Sounding Patterns)

### Name the Industry's Problems Out Loud
Reddit users complain about specific things. Mirror their language:
- "COAs with no lot number"
- "Available upon request" (and then they never send one)
- "Ship thousands of vials while sampling only a single unit"
- "We don't grade our own homework"
- "Not our cousin's friend's lab"

### Use Colloquial Idioms
- "We don't grade our own homework."
- "Not our cousin's friend's lab."
- "Same day means same day."
- "No 'available upon request' BS."

### Short Declarative Sentences
- "No faith required."
- "Every single lot."
- "Same day means same day."
- Period. Not exclamation marks. Not question marks.

### Direct FAQ Answers
Start answers with a real word:
- ✅ "Yes — every batch."
- ✅ "No. Absolutely not."
- ✅ "Absolutely."
- ❌ "Great question! At PRG, we pride ourselves on..."

### Address Real Customer Anxieties
Build FAQ entries around literal questions support gets:
- "Why does my peptide bottle appear empty?"
- "Difficulty extracting product from vials?"
- "Can I get a refund if I already opened it?"

### Be Specific, Not Generic
- ❌ "High purity"
- ✅ "99%+ purity, HPLC verified, lot number on the COA matches the vial"

- ❌ "Fast shipping"
- ✅ "Order before 2PM CST, it goes out today"

- ❌ "Secure checkout"
- ✅ "Crypto payments go straight to our wallet. No middleman."

### Leave Slightly-Imperfect Phrasing
Over-editing kills the human signal. If a sentence is grammatically awkward but reads like a person wrote it, leave it. AI would have cleaned it up.

### Sign Updates Like a Human
Limitless Biotech signs FAQ updates: "Gratefully, The Limitless Biotech Team." Tiny touch. Big human signal.

## Tone Summary

- **Voice**: Direct, no-nonsense, slightly cynical about the industry
- **Tone**: Confident but not boastful. Specific, not generic.
- **Pronouns**: "We" for the company. "You" for the customer. Avoid "one" or passive voice.
- **Contractions**: Use them. "Don't" not "Do not." "We're" not "We are."
- **Exclamation marks**: Rarely. Periods are stronger.
- **Question marks**: Only for actual questions. Not for rhetorical flourish.

## Disclaimer Copy

Reddit users have read "FOR RESEARCH USE ONLY - NOT FOR HUMAN OR VETERINARY USE" ten thousand times. They tune it out.

Better approach (from Cosmic Peptides):
> "Do you provide syringes, needles, or dosing/administration guidance? No. Absolutely not. We do not sell syringes, needles, or any injection supplies... To be completely clear: Our products are lyophilized reference materials for in vitro laboratory research only. They are not drugs. They are not for injection. They are not for consumption. Any inquiry suggesting intended human use, self-administration, or therapeutic application is a direct violation of our Terms of Service."

That's a disclaimer that also functions as a trust signal. A scammer serving human-use customers wouldn't write this.
